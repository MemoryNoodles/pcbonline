Flexible PCB Overview

  • A flexible PCB, or FPC, is a thin, light, and bendable PCB made up of alternating layers of flex copper-clad laminate (FCCL) and polyimide (PI) or polyester (PET) layers. Stiffeners (PI, FR4, or stainless steel) can be added to flexible PCBs for connection with other system parts.
  • A flex PCB can be single-sided or dual-sided, single-layered, double-layered, or multilayer. And the FPC color can be yellow, black, white, or transparent. The PI or PET material is not only the substrate but also the coverlay that equals the solder mask of rigid PCBs. And an FCCL is made by bonding copper foil and PI coated with adhesive, or dip-coating copper on PI, or coating and curing liquid PI on copper foil. The FCCL made from the last two methods, without adhesive, is thinner and has better reliability.
  • If your flexible PCBs are to be frequently bent or twisted, ask the FPC manufacturer to use rolled annealed (RA) copper for production. If your flexible PCBs to be used do not require much flexibility, you can choose the cheaper electrolytic copper for flexible PCB production.

Flexible Printed Circut Board Assembly

Flexible PCB assembly does not need special assembly technologies but requires jigs to fix them throughout the whole assembly process.
The assembly technique of flexible PCBs is also surface-mount technology (SMT) as rigid PCBs do. But flexible PCB assembly has these differences:
● Before the SMT assembly, flexible PCBs need baking to remove the moisture that the FPC materials absorb.
● During the assembly process, flexible circuit boards need to be fixed on a rigid surface for dimensional stability.
● The reflow temperatures for flexible PCBs are within 300°C, lower than that for rigid PCBs.
PCBONLINE provides PCB assembly for all types of flex PCBs, and fine-pitch assembly is available. The minimum SMD footprint is 01005. The COB LEDs can be mounted by flip-chip or wire bonding on flexible PCBs.

Applications and Benefits of Flexible PCBs

  • Flexible printed circuit boards are widely used in wearable electronic products, automotive LED lights, medical equipment, foldable LED screens, lithium battery covers, etc. Transparent flex PCBs can be used for more state-of-the-art applications, such as biological growth trays through which you can observe the cells inside. And the advantages of flexible PCBs include:
  • ● Flexible PCBs are thin and light and reduce the electronic products by 40% to 60% of space and 60% to 80% of weight. They meet the trend of thin-line and high-density.
  • ● Flexible PCBs are bendable, foldable, and twistable, and can move and stretch in 3D space to achieve integration of assembly and connection.
  • ● FPCs without adhesive maintain good tear strength and have little size changes (within 0.1%) at a high temperature of 300°C, and have better resistance to chemicals than FPCs with adhesive.
